The Indian Cancer Society – Cancer Cure Fund provides financial aid for underprivileged cancer patients across India, ensuring timely treatment for cancers that are often curable when detected early. Since its launch, the project has helped thousands return to healthy, productive lives, with 45% of beneficiaries aged 16–45. This first-of-its-kind initiative was created in partnership with HDFC Mutual Fund, which in 2011 introduced a closed-ended debt fund allowing investors to donate 50% or up to 75% of their dividend income to ICS. The generous contributions of these investors, along with HDFC Mutua

Disbursed Rs. 302.72 crores for the treatment of more than 16,000+ patients since inception till March'25.

Patient eligibility criteria


  • Family income should not exceed Rs 4 lakh per year.
  • Patient should be treated at ICS empanelled hospital only. 
  • Patients should be registered under general category.
  • Projected survival rate 50% for adult patients and 60% for pediatric patients.
  • Only for Indian citizens.

Sanction criteria


Maximum sanction Rs 5 lakhs and for Bone Marrow
Transplant Rs.8 lakh.

Treatment protocol covered


  • Chemotherapy
  • Radiation
  • Surgery
  • Supportive care
  • Prosthesis

Arun Kurkure Initiation and Treatment Fund (AKITF)

This project was launched in April 2014 in Mumbai to support underprivileged cancer patients towards initial diagnostic tests and funding for emergency treatment, without any loss of time.

Many patients come to a cancer hospital, but are unable to do the diagnostic tests due to lack of funds. They go back home and return to the hospital when the cancer is in an advanced stage, making treatment difficult.

This immediate assistance is a big help to patients, it reduces the drop rate of patients and allows them to start treatment giving them time to look for other sources of assistance.

The Project was re-launched on 4th Feb 2019 at our empanelled hospitals.


Patient eligibility criteria


  • Indian citizens only

  • No discrimination on account of age, sex or religion

  • The patient must be registered as a general ward patient 

  • Family income not exceeding Rs 4 lakhs per annum

  • Initial treatment will be applicable only for cancer diagnosed patients

Documentation from patient


  • Aadhar card of the patient (if pediatric patient, then Aadhar
    card of the one of the parents)

  • Income proof that patient’s family income is not exceeding
    Rs 4 lakhs per annum 

  • Registration page confirming patient belonging to general
    category of the empanelled hospital 

  • Prescription of the treating doctor for initial diagnosis 

  • Cost certificate for initial treatment from the treating doctor

  • Additional documents for initial treatment: All cancer reviewed
    and attached to the documentation by the hospital

  • Photo of the patient

Maximum of Rs 25,000 - per patient is sanctioned for initial diagnosis & initial treatment. Disbursed Rs. 18.87 crores for the initial investigation and treatment of more than 13,000+ patients since relaunch till March'25.
